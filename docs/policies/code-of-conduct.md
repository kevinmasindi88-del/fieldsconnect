# FieldsConnect Code of Conduct

Version: 0.1 (MVP draft)

Effective date: To be approved before external beta testing.

## 1. Purpose

FieldsConnect exists to help students, professionals, startups, institutions, mentors, and collaborators connect, learn, share knowledge, and build opportunities. Every user is expected to participate respectfully, honestly, safely, and professionally.

This Code of Conduct applies to all FieldsConnect activity, including profiles, profile images, posts, comments, messages, skills, ratings, library resources, notifications, reports, and any future project or collaboration workspace.

## 2. Expected conduct

Users must:

- communicate respectfully and professionally;
- engage in good faith and avoid deliberate disruption;
- provide honest profile, skill, experience, and institutional information;
- respect privacy, confidentiality, intellectual property, and consent;
- use reporting tools responsibly;
- comply with applicable law and FieldsConnect platform rules;
- cooperate with reasonable moderation decisions and investigations.

## 3. Prohibited conduct

### 3.1 Harassment, bullying, and intimidation

Users may not repeatedly insult, target, humiliate, threaten, intimidate, stalk, or pressure another person. Disagreement and criticism are permitted when expressed respectfully and without personal abuse.

### 3.2 Hate, discrimination, and degrading conduct

Users may not attack, degrade, exclude, or promote hostility toward people based on protected or personal characteristics, including race, ethnicity, nationality, religion, sex, gender, disability, age, or sexual orientation.

### 3.3 Threats, violence, and dangerous conduct

Users may not make credible threats, encourage violence, promote dangerous conduct, or use the platform to coordinate harm.

### 3.4 Sexual, exploitative, or inappropriate content

Users may not publish sexually explicit, exploitative, predatory, or otherwise inappropriate content. Content involving minors is subject to the strictest possible action and may be escalated where legally required.

### 3.5 Spam, scams, fraud, and misleading content

Users may not send repetitive unsolicited material, operate scams, impersonate others, manipulate users, misrepresent qualifications, fabricate opportunities, or deliberately publish materially misleading information.

### 3.6 Privacy and confidentiality violations

Users may not disclose another person's private, personal, confidential, medical, employment, academic, financial, or identifying information without lawful authority or consent.

### 3.7 Intellectual property and confidential materials

Users may not upload, copy, distribute, or claim ownership of content they do not have the right to use. Confidential business, academic, technical, or institutional information must not be shared without authority.

### 3.8 Abusive or inappropriate platform content

The following may be moderated when they violate this Code:

- profile names, biographies, qualifications, and profile images;
- posts and comments;
- direct messages;
- skills, ratings, and endorsements;
- library documents and public resources;
- future project, bidding, collaboration, or institutional content.

### 3.9 Manipulation and platform abuse

Users may not manipulate ratings, create deceptive accounts, evade sanctions, interfere with moderation, exploit technical vulnerabilities, or abuse platform features.

### 3.10 Malicious or false reporting

Users must not knowingly submit fabricated, retaliatory, repetitive, or malicious reports. A report made in good faith will not be penalised merely because no violation is found.

## 4. Reporting concerns

Users may report a profile or specific content through FieldsConnect moderation tools. Reports should identify the relevant person or item and provide enough information for a fair review.

The identity of the reporter should not be disclosed to the reported user except where legally required or necessary for due process.

Submitting a report does not itself prove a violation. Moderators must review the available evidence and apply this Code consistently.

## 5. Moderation outcomes

Depending on severity, context, history, and risk, FieldsConnect may:

- dismiss a report where no violation is found;
- provide guidance or an informal notice;
- issue a formal warning;
- redact or remove a post, comment, message, profile image, skill entry, rating, or library resource;
- restrict specific account functions;
- suspend an account for 12 days;
- suspend an account for 30 days;
- temporarily suspend an account pending senior review;
- permanently terminate an account for severe or repeated violations;
- preserve evidence or escalate a matter where legally required.

FieldsConnect is not required to apply every step in sequence. Severe conduct may result in immediate suspension or termination.

## 6. Redaction notices

When content is removed from public or shared view, FieldsConnect may replace it with a neutral notice, including:

- "This post has been removed for violating the FieldsConnect Code of Conduct."
- "This message has been redacted following a moderation review."
- "This public resource has been removed for violating the FieldsConnect Code of Conduct."
- "This profile image has been removed following a moderation review."

The original content may be retained privately for audit, appeal, legal, or safety purposes.

## 7. Warnings and suspensions

A warning may state:

> Your content was reported and found to violate the FieldsConnect Code of Conduct. The content has been removed or redacted. Further offences may result in account suspension or termination.

Temporary suspension periods used in the MVP are:

- 12 days for moderate or repeated misconduct;
- 30 days for serious or repeated misconduct;
- temporary suspension pending review where risk or evidence requires escalation.

Permanent termination may be applied for severe violations, repeated violations after prior sanctions, sanction evasion, serious fraud, exploitation, credible threats, or other conduct that creates unacceptable risk.

## 8. Fair review principles

Moderators must:

- review evidence impartially;
- apply the Code consistently;
- record the reason for each decision;
- declare and avoid conflicts of interest;
- protect reporter and user confidentiality;
- avoid punishment solely because a report was filed;
- use the least severe action that reasonably protects users and the platform;
- escalate severe, uncertain, or high-risk matters to a senior moderator or admin.

## 9. Moderator identity and accountability

User-facing moderation communications will be sent under the shared name **FCModerators**. Individual moderator names will not ordinarily be disclosed to users.

Internally, FieldsConnect must record the actual moderator account, ticket reference, action, policy basis, notes, and timestamps for audit purposes.

## 10. Moderation tickets

Each report should receive a traceable ticket number in the format:

`FC-MOD-YY-NNNNNN`

Example: `FC-MOD-26-000001`.

The final six-digit sequence restarts at `000001` each calendar year. The full ticket reference must remain unique.

## 11. Notifications

The reporter should receive confirmation that the report was received and a closure notice when the case is completed.

The reported user should receive an appropriate notice where action is taken. Moderator messages should identify the relevant content type, reported reason, decision, and possible consequences of further violations without revealing the reporter's identity.

## 12. Appeals

Users should be able to request review of serious moderation decisions, particularly suspensions and permanent termination. Appeals must be reviewed by a person who did not make the original decision where reasonably possible.

An appeal does not automatically reverse or pause a safety-related restriction.

## 13. Moderator nominations and privileged roles

Moderator, senior moderator, and admin rights are not available during general signup.

Privileged roles are assigned only after:

1. a justified nomination;
2. a private invitation to the candidate;
3. review of the Code of Conduct and relevant role brief;
4. acceptance of confidentiality, impartiality, conflict-of-interest, and accountability obligations;
5. final approval and activation by an authorised admin.

The role progression is:

`User -> Moderator -> Senior Moderator -> Admin`

Acceptance of a nomination does not by itself activate privileges. Every nomination, approval, activation, promotion, revocation, and role change must be logged.

## 14. Changes to this Code

FieldsConnect may update this Code as the platform develops. Material changes should be versioned, dated, and communicated to users. Where appropriate, users may be required to acknowledge an updated version before continuing to use affected services.

## 15. Acceptance

By creating or continuing to use a FieldsConnect account, users agree to follow this Code of Conduct together with the FieldsConnect Terms and Conditions and Privacy Policy.
